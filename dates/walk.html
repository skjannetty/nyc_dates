Details

Here are some walking route options in Manhattan with recommended eateries along each route:

  • The High Line

    You got a sneak peek, but we could start at the entrance to the High Line at Gansevoort Street and walk the length of the park to 34th Street. The High Line features art installations (including one by Ellen's uncle!), gardens, and views of the Hudson River.

    Internet Eatery Recommendations:

    • Artichoke Basille's Pizza - Famous for their artichoke pizza, located nearby at 114 10th Ave.
    • Cookshop - Farm-to-table cuisine, located at 156 10th Ave.

  • Central Park

    Lets be real, there's no way we go a trip to NYC without walking around Central Park. We could make it really featured though if we so choose (and perhaps run into an unexpected friend by the sea lion exhibit...)

    Internet Eatery Recommendations:

    • East Village Pizza - What theme? There's no theme. These restaurants were chosen with full objectivity.
    • Liberty Bagels - Near the Apple store, these are the guys that do the rainbow bagels. All their bagels are genuinely very good though I got a lox bagel here last time I was in NYC and it was delightful.

  • Brooklyn Bridge to DUMBO

    I don't think I've ever walked across the Brooklyn Bridge. We could start at City Hall Park, cross the Brooklyn Bridge, and explore the DUMBO area. We could even combine this with the brewery tour walk idea and hit up some of the great breweries in that area.

    Internet Eatery Recommendations:

    • Grimaldi's Pizzeria - Famous for their coal-fired brick-oven pizza, located at 1 Front St. Still don't know what theme you're talking about.
    • Time Out Market - A food market with a variety of options, located at 55 Water St. This place always looks good but also extremely chaotic. It reminds me of that food market we ate at just outside Tivoli.

  • West Village to Greenwich Village

    These are also areas I don't know well. We would start our walk at Washington Square Park and explore.

    Internet Eatery Recommendations:

    • Joe's Pizza - A classic NYC pizza spot located at 7 Carmine St.
    • Auntie Guan's Kitchen - A great spot for Chinese Food.
    • Marie's Crisis Cafe - This is a piano bar that everyone says devolves into Broadway singalong as the night goes on. I really think we should check it out at some point!

These ideas are obviously not exhaustive.
